# The Producer-Observer Seam: Joining Spectral Brand Theory and Organizational Schema Theory at Meaning, Meaningfulness, and the Perception Cloud

Dmitry Zharnikov

ORCID: 0009-0000-6893-9231

DOI: [10.5281/zenodo.21114525](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.21114525)

Working Paper v1.0.0 – July 2026

## Abstract

Two frameworks in this research program draw the same boundary from opposite sides without naming it. Organizational Schema Theory's six-tier producer stack terminates at the rendered Tier-4 instance and hands off to the observer perceptual filter; the meaning/meaningfulness cost-asymmetry framework's pipeline — substrate to meaning to rendered instance — ends at exactly the artifact that filter consumes. This note makes the seam explicit. The producer side owns substrate, meaning, and rendering (Tiers 1 through 4); the observer side owns the projection of the rendered instance into a perception cloud; and the meaning/meaningfulness/perception triad is a join of the two frameworks, not a slice of either alone. Three results follow: a seam map with two honesty guards (meaningfulness is multi-tier in general; perception is definitionally outside the tier stack); a flattening parametrization in which the triad attaches to whatever tier a business's value concentrates toward, with software as the limit existence proof; and an endogenous-intent closure in which perception clouds sediment into the next generation's substrate through human transducers, yielding a measurable loop-health diagnostic: perception-cloud variance over time.

**Keywords:** perception cloud, meaning, meaningfulness, organizational tiers, brand specification, cultural transmission, observer completion, model collapse

---

A theory that says what a firm *is* and a theory that says how a firm is *perceived* must meet somewhere, and where they meet is worth naming precisely. In this research program the two theories are already published and already compatible: Organizational Schema Theory (OST) [@zharnikov-2026-organizational-schema-theory-test-driven] describes the producer-side object stack — Owner Intent, Business Model, Business Entity, Product, Process, Organization — through which a firm renders artifacts into the world [@zharnikov-2026-dual-hierarchies-organizational-transferability-six]; Spectral Brand Theory (SBT) describes how any rendered artifact is completed into perception by observers whose spectral profiles amplify, attenuate, or invert what the artifact emits [@zharnikov-2026-spectral-brand-theory-computational-framework]. Between them sits a third construction: the meaning/meaningfulness hierarchy, in which an extraction operator $\sigma$ takes the recorded working log $L$ to a transmissible semantic structure $S$ (meaning), and a rendering operator $\rho$ takes $(S, \text{cohort}, \text{language}, \text{medium})$ to a prose realization $R$ (meaningfulness) — two separately optimizable layers of one knowledge artifact [@zharnikov-2026ao-spec-based-research-post-ai].

The claim of this note is that these are not three adjacent theories but one pipeline seen from two ends, and that the hand-off point — the *producer-observer seam* — is already drawn in the parent texts: explicitly, in so many words, on the producer side; by scope construction on the meaning side. Making the seam explicit buys three things. First, a *seam map*: a statement of which framework owns which stretch of the pipeline, with the two guards that keep the map honest. Second, a *flattening parametrization*: the meaning/meaningfulness/perception triad attaches not to a fixed tier but to whatever tier a given business's value concentrates toward, and the software firm — where the Process and Organization tiers approach zero — is the limit case that proves the attachment exists. Third, an *endogenous-intent closure*: once the seam is drawn, the loop closes through the human who stands on both sides of it, and Owner Intent stops being an exogenous primitive.

This is deliberately a short bridge note, not a standalone theory paper. Its novelty is the join itself and what the join makes visible; every proposition here is either absent from both parent papers or an explicit application of one of them. The one new falsifiable commitment is the variance diagnostic of the final section.

## The Seam Map

***The hand-off, stated from both sides***

The producer-side framework already names its own terminus. The brand-as-Tier-4 result states it twice, in ontological and then in operational form: "The brand is *not* the customer's perception of that specification; the perception lives downstream, after the projection through the observer perceptual filter," and "The brand-as-Tier-4 specification is the firm's controllable input; the *perception cloud* is the cohort-specific output" [@zharnikov-2026-brand-as-modular-layer-tiered]. The tier stack, in other words, ends at a rendered, customer-facing instance and explicitly cedes what happens next to an observer-side theory. OST has no observer tier — and this is by design, not omission.

The meaning/meaningfulness framework ends at the same artifact from the other direction. Its published pipeline runs $L \to S \to R$: the log is the recorded working material, meaning $S$ is the transmissible structure the extraction $\sigma$ recovers from it, and meaningfulness $R$ is the rendered realization produced for a cohort, language, and medium [@zharnikov-2026ao-spec-based-research-post-ai]. Nothing in that framework models what a reader *makes of* $R$; the reader's completion is outside its boundary conditions by design, exactly as perception is outside the tier stack's scope. This note adds one construct of its own at the upstream end: call the working material *below* the log — the accumulated experience and intent from which the log is drawn — the *substrate*. The substrate sits loosely at Tier-1 Owner Intent; it is this note's extension, not the parent's published layer, and it earns its place in the closing sections, where the loop returns to it.

SBT picks up precisely there. The observer's spectral profile projects the rendered instance into a perception; over a cohort of observers those per-observer completions aggregate into the perception cloud — one may picture a foam of per-observer perception bubbles whose collective surface, seen from a distance, is the cohort's cloud, though the canonical term, used everywhere hereafter, is perception cloud [@zharnikov-2026-spectral-brand-theory-computational-framework]. Writing $\Pi$ for this observer projection, the full pipeline is:

$$\text{substrate} \to L \to S \to \rho(S) = R \to \Pi(R) = \text{perception cloud}$$

with the producer side owning $\text{substrate} \to L \to S \to R$ (Tiers 1 through 4: the substrate loosely at Tier-1 Owner Intent, meaning as the transmissible producer specification concentrated at Tier 4) and SBT owning $R \to \Pi(R)$ (the observer side, outside the tier stack). There is no overlap — neither framework claims the other's stretch — and no gap: the rendered instance that terminates one is the input that begins the other. That the hand-off is clean is not this note's construction. On the OST side it is textual — the sentences quoted above name the terminus and cede perception downstream; on the meaning side it is structural — the pipeline terminates at $R$ and places the reader's completion outside its boundary conditions by design. What is new here is only the naming of the seam and what follows from naming it.

**Proposition 1 (the join).** *The producer side (substrate through rendered instance, Tiers 1 through 4) and the observer side (rendered instance through perception cloud) are stretches of one pipeline; their hand-off at the rendered instance is clean — no overlap, no gap; and the meaning/meaningfulness/perception triad is a join of the two frameworks, not a slice of either alone.*

***The join, not a slice***

Two guards keep the seam map honest, and both must be stated as scope rather than buried as caveats.

The first guard concerns meaningfulness. It is tempting to read the triad as a single-tier object — meaning is the Tier-4 specification, meaningfulness is the rendered Tier-4 instance — and in the flattened limit of the next section that reading is exact. In the general six-tier firm it is false: the rendered experience a customer receives is co-produced by the Tier-4 specification *with* Tier-5 process (delivery, service, returns) and Tier-6 organization (who made it, and how that shows), while Tier-2 business model and Tier-3 entity standing leak in as felt relationship and legitimacy. Meaningfulness is $\rho$ evaluated across the rendered *stack*, not across one tier of it. A bridge drawn without this guard would over-claim, and the over-claim would be structural, not cosmetic.

The second guard concerns the word "slice." Two of the triad's three members — meaning and meaningfulness — are producer-internal; the third — perception — is definitionally outside the tier stack. So the triad is not a slice of OST, and it is not a slice of SBT either, which has no producer interior. It is a *join*: the smallest structure that contains the seam and one stretch of pipeline on each side of it. This is a stronger statement than a within-framework slice would be, because it identifies the precise point at which a firm-internal theory must hand off to an observer theory, and shows the two existing frameworks already meet there without modification. The absence of an observer tier in OST is the insertion point for SBT, not a defect in either.

Three external traditions sit closest to the seam, and each relates to it differently. The theory of cultural meaning movement states the movement without the decomposition: meaning travels from the culturally constituted world into goods and from goods into consumers via named instruments — advertising, fashion, ritual [@mccracken-1986-culture-consumption-theoretical]; the seam map agrees with the direction and adds the formal split into a producer stretch (specifiable, versionable, tier-decomposed) and an observer stretch (projective, cohort-resolved, measurable as a cloud), with the hand-off artifact identified. The communication-theoretic ancestor is the encoding/decoding model [@hall-1980-encodingdecoding-s-hall], with which the seam agrees that reception is polysemic and co-authored; what encoding/decoding does not carry is precisely what the join supplies — a tiered producer interior, a cohort-resolved measurement of the observer side, and the tier parametrization of the next section. And brand co-creation theory [@hatch-2010-toward-theory-brand] does not lack the boundary but *denies* it, reading brand meaning as co-authored across a porous firm-stakeholder line. The seam map concedes the substance of that objection — observer completions really do flow back into production, and the endogenous-intent closure below makes the feedback structural — while keeping the layers distinct within any one turn: co-creation operates *through* the loop, as sediment returning to substrate, not by erasing the specification/perception distinction at the moment of rendering.

## The Flattening Parametrization

***Software as the limit case***

Consider a software firm. Its Process tier and Organization tier, as customer-facing co-producers of the rendered experience, approach zero: the artifact executes in an environment that adds essentially no operational or organizational layer between the built product and the user. What remains of the six-tier stack is the coder's intent (Tier 1), the source (Tier 4 as specification), the built application at an exact version (Tier 4 as rendered instance), and the user's perception cloud of the running application. In the pipeline's vocabulary: source code is meaning $S$; the deterministic build is $\rho$; the built artifact is meaningfulness $R$; the user's experience is $\Pi(R)$.

Software is therefore the *existence proof* for the triad's tier attachment. In the flattened limit there is nothing else left in the stack for the triad to be confused with — the middle tiers are pinched to zero, the C2 guard's multi-tier co-production vanishes, and meaning/meaningfulness/perception is exactly the {specification, rendered instance, observer perception} slice of Tier 4 with Tier-1 intent upstream. The limit case is what licenses the general claim without over-extending it.

***Different businesses flatten toward different tiers***

Software is *a* limit, not *the* limit. The tier-separability results of the brand-as-modular-layer framework already establish that firms differ in which tiers their value binds to [@zharnikov-2026-brand-as-modular-layer-tiered]; the parametrization extends that observation to the triad. A brand-led consumer firm concentrates value at Tier 4, and its triad is the software triad with the C2 guard restored. A process-moat engineering firm — the classic hidden-champion configuration — concentrates value at Tier 5: its *meaning* is a codified process specification, its *meaningfulness* is the executed process, and its consumer-facing perception cloud may be negligible or absent, because business-to-business process moats often have no consumer observers at all.

The general form of the claim is therefore parametric, and it is the section's proposition.

**Proposition 2 (flattening).** *The meaning/meaningfulness/perception triad is the {specification, rendered specification, observer perception} slice of the dominant tier a given business's value concentrates toward, with Tier-1 intent always upstream: Tier 4 for brand-led and software firms, Tier 5 for process-moat firms — where the observer side may be empty.*

The parametrization bounds the bridge — it says where the single-tier reading holds, where it must be re-attached, and where the observer side may be empty — and in doing so converts the seam map from an elegant special case into a scoped general statement.

## Levels of Reading

Read observer-side, the pipeline's stack induces a model of reading depth. An observer of the same rendered artifact may stop at their own completion — the perception, co-authored by their spectral profile and context; may reach the *meaningfulness* — the rendered work as an object with properties of its own; may reach the *meaning* — the recoverable specification, the structure any faithful rendering preserves; or may reach toward the *substrate* — the upstream intent and working material below the work. Call this four-level model the *levels of reading*, and call the deepest level an observer's extraction currently reaches their *observer depth*. Ascending the levels is progressively decentering: away from the observer's own projection, toward the work itself.

Observer depth is not a metaphor; it is operationalizable. A reading at meaning-depth is precisely one whose extraction recovers the source specification — so depth is measurable as coverage of the source spine recovered by the observer's extraction, and it increases with observer maturation. This connects the seam map to the rendering-equivalence result: two renderings of one locked specification converge on conclusions if and only if both preserve the specification's structural elements [@zharnikov-2026ap-same-meaning-different-prose]. The meaning-depth reading is exactly the reading positioned to check that equivalence; a perception-depth reading, by construction, cannot distinguish a faithful rendering from a broken one, because it never touches the layer where fidelity is defined.

**Proposition 3 (observer depth).** *Observers of one rendered artifact differ in the deepest level their extraction reaches — perception, meaningfulness, meaning, substrate — and that depth is measurable as coverage of the source specification recovered by the extraction, increasing with observer maturation.*

## The Endogenous-Intent Closure

***The transducer***

Both parent frameworks quietly treat intent as exogenous: OST takes Tier-1 Owner Intent as given, and the meaning/meaningfulness framework begins from a substrate that is simply there. The seam map exposes why neither needs to keep that assumption. Follow the pipeline past its observer end: a perception cloud does not evaporate when the encounter ends. It settles. The observer who inhabited it carries its sediment forward, and when that observer later *forms an intent* — founds a firm, specifies a product, wants a yacht — the intent is formed out of exactly that sediment. The owner intends a yacht because, as a prior observer, they inhabited a perception cloud in which yachts encode status. Tier-1 intent is the point at which an accumulated perception cloud is internalized as individual purpose.

The loop therefore closes through the human: the same person is receiver of perception on one turn and former of intent on the next, producer and consumer being one population wearing two hats. Perception clouds of generation $N$ sediment into the substrate of generation $N{+}1$; substrate yields meaning; meaning renders; renderings project into new clouds. Intent is a *fixed point of this loop*, not an exogenous primitive — and the loop is the same structure at every scale, from one reading, to one biography, to cultural succession across generations.

The formal machinery for the sedimentation step exists in cultural-evolution theory, which models socially transmitted preference and belief formation as an inheritance system with its own transmission dynamics — including guided variation, in which individual learning biases what is transmitted onward, the mechanism onto which the closure's judgment term maps [@boyd-richerson-1985-culture-evolutionary-process]. And the loop's brand-level trace has been documented from the practitioner side: iconic brands succeed precisely by recycling accumulated cultural material into new brand meaning [@holt-2004-how-brands-become]. What the closure adds to both is the producer/observer decomposition: cultural transmission tells us sediment moves; the cultural-branding circuit tells us brands mine it; the seam map says *where in the firm's object stack* the mined sediment re-enters — at Tier 1 — and through *which artifact* it was deposited — the perception cloud of a prior rendering. The one-directional movement of meaning from world to good to consumer [@mccracken-1986-culture-consumption-theoretical] becomes, with the return edge named, a loop.

***Two guards: horizon and judgment***

The closure invites two objections, and each is met by a guard stated here as scope. The first is infinite regress: if every intent traces to prior perception, analysis never bottoms out. The answer is the analysis horizon: the substrate is locked at a chosen depth — the same lock the meaning/meaningfulness framework already imposes on its own substrate layer — and the closure reframes that lock as a chosen horizon rather than a true primitive. Nothing requires tracing Tolstoy's substrate to the dawn of writing; everything requires admitting the substrate *has* upstream structure the analyst chose not to open.

The second is determinism: if perception sediments into intent, people would only ever re-emit what they absorbed. But the substrate includes more than perceptual history — biology, material conditions, accident — and, decisively, the human contributes *judgment*: a novelty term applied at the moment sediment becomes purpose. Perception shapes intent without determining it. The judgment term is why the loop is a spiral that expands rather than a circle that repeats — and it is exactly this term whose fate the final section makes measurable.

**Proposition 4 (endogenous intent).** *Tier-1 owner intent is a fixed point of a human-mediated, scale-invariant loop in which perception clouds sediment into next-generation substrate through transducers who are both receivers of perception and formers of intent — bounded at a chosen analysis horizon, with judgment as the irreducible novelty term.*

## The Variance Diagnostic

The closure acquires empirical teeth when machine observers enter the loop. Machines already occupy the renderer position (generative production), increasingly occupy the perceiver position (models that read, summarize, recommend, and buy), and in agentic configurations begin to occupy the intent-forming position. Each occupied position replaces a human transducer — and the human transducer, in the closure's terms, is the loop's variance source: the judgment term that injects genuinely new structure each turn.

Three independent results, at three different levels, say what happens when the variance source is removed. At the training level, models trained on recursively generated data degrade — the model-collapse result [@shumailov-2024-ai-models-collapse]. At the output level, aligned language models homogenize: across open-ended tasks their responses concentrate far more tightly than human responses [@jiang-2025-artificial-hivemind], with the homogenization localized substantially to the alignment step [@liu-2026-alignment-tax-response]. And at the brand level, this program's own instrument finds that machine observers dimensionally collapse brand perception to a low-dimensional readout [@zharnikov-2026-dimensional-collapse-ai-mediated-search]. These are separate findings at separate levels, cited here separately; the synthesis — that all three are the same variance sink observed at different stations of one loop — is this note's, and it yields the note's one new falsifiable commitment.

*The diagnostic.* As machine observers occupy more loop positions, track perception-cloud variance over time. A closed text-in/text-out machine loop is a variance sink, so a monotone shrink of cohort spread — a widening machine-versus-human flattening gap — indicates an autophagic loop: sediment recycling without novelty injection. The steelman is built in: a machine coupled to reality with exploratory reward can be a variance *source*, so the diagnostic tests loop health, not machine presence.

Two predictions follow, and they are the note's one new falsifiable commitment.

**Proposition 5 (variance diagnostic).** *Loop health is measurable as perception-cloud variance over time: (i) a brand category's cloud variance at time $T$ predicts the diversity of successor specifications at $T{+}1$; (ii) categories with rising machine-observer share show declining cloud variance relative to human-observer baselines — a monotone shrink indicating an autophagic loop.*

Both predictions run on instruments this program already operates; neither is executed here. The note's contribution is to derive the diagnostic from the closure and commit to it as the falsifiable shadow of what would otherwise remain a meditative claim.

## Scope: What This Note Does Not Claim

A word first on the unit of analysis, since the note's claims live at three scales. The primary unit is *one turn of the pipeline around a single rendered artifact*: the producer-side tiers are properties of the firm that renders the artifact, the perception cloud is a property of the (artifact, cohort) pair, and the seam is crossed once per turn. The flattening parametrization evaluates the *firm* — which tier its value concentrates toward. The closure and the variance diagnostic aggregate *many turns* at brand-category scale, across generations of artifacts and observers. Claims at one scale do not automatically transfer to another; each proposition names its own.

Three boundaries, corresponding to the three guards above, fix what has *not* been claimed. The single-tier reading of the triad holds only where value concentrates at Tier 4; elsewhere the parametrized form applies, and for process-moat firms the observer side may be empty. Meaningfulness is single-tier only in the flattened limit; in general it is the projection of the whole rendered stack. And the endogenous-intent closure captures the socially transmitted component of intent under a chosen analysis horizon, with judgment retained as an irreducible novelty term — it is an analytical device for making Tier 1 tractable, not a causal theory of human motivation. Within those boundaries, the seam map, the parametrization, the levels of reading, the closure, and the variance diagnostic are offered as the connective tissue between two published frameworks — a join, named at last, rather than a third framework competing with its own parents.
