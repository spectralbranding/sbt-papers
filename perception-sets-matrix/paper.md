# Perception Sets the Matrix: Spectral Match as the Generative Layer of Stochastic Brand-Choice Propensities

Dmitry Zharnikov

ORCID: 0009-0000-6893-9231

DOI: [10.5281/zenodo.21319716](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.21319716)

Working Paper Draft – July 2026 (pre-registered campaign executed 2026-07-12)

## Abstract

The stochastic brand-choice tradition — Markov switching models and the NBD-Dirichlet — earns its descriptive success by taking brand-choice propensities as exogenous givens: the models locate the equilibrium the propensities imply and are silent on what sets them. This paper supplies and tests the missing generative layer. At cohort level, spectral match — a similarity statistic between an observer cohort's eight-dimensional perception profile and a brand's profile — is proposed as a monotone predictor of the cohort's stated choice propensity within its category consideration set. A propensity vector induces the zero-order Dirichlet switching matrix in closed form. The perceptual layer therefore sets the matrix the tradition starts from: the double-jeopardy diagonal is inherited, the intermediate-propensity band becomes model-derived, and perceptually metameric brand pairs must receive statistically indistinguishable propensity vectors — a prediction no utility-based or congruity-based account makes. In a pre-registered LLM-cohort campaign under strict instrument separation with four kill conditions, the link held experimentally (τ_b = .323, p < .001; a random-profile placebo destroys it), replicated in quick-service coffee, failed in athletic footwear, and showed no common-method inflation (Δτ = −.002); metameric brand pairs violated propensity equality, bounding the perceptual layer's sufficiency exactly where real brand profiles converge.

**Keywords:** brand-choice propensity, NBD-Dirichlet, switching matrix, spectral match, self-congruity, processing fluency, preregistration, LLM observers

---

Sixty years of stochastic brand-choice modeling rest on a deliberate silence. The Markov appraisal of brand switching took the switching table itself as the modeling object [@ehrenberg-1965-appraisal-markov-brand-switching]; the NBD-Dirichlet completed the program by treating each buyer's brand-choice propensities as steady, heterogeneous, Dirichlet-distributed givens and deriving the category's equilibrium regularities from them [@goodhardt-1984-dirichlet-comprehensive-model]. The program succeeded on its own terms: double jeopardy, duplication-of-purchase, and the benchmark tables reproduce observed category structure with famous reliability [@ehrenberg-1990-double-jeopardy-revisited; @ehrenberg-2004-understanding-brand-performance]. Its modern statement remains the reference account of how brands grow [@sharp-2010-how-brands-grow]. But the propensities themselves enter as inputs. The tradition acknowledges that they differ across buyers and across brands, models the heterogeneity distributionally, and does not explain it — a silence its founders chose, and one this corpus has recently sharpened: the purchase-level switching table of the zero-order Dirichlet is fast-mixing with no slow modes, so whatever structure the propensities carry is set upstream of the switching process, not generated by it [@zharnikov-2026-restoring-perceptual-separability-after-coherence]. This paper asks where those inputs come from, and answers with a measurement model: perception sets them.

This paper models and pre-registers a test of that upstream layer. The proposal is a spectral-match linking model with three components. First, a *match statistic*: cohort-level perceptual similarity $m(c,b)$ between an observer cohort's spectral profile and a brand's profile, both vectors on the eight-dimensional perception space of Spectral Brand Theory — eight dimensions of brand meaning, Semiotic through Temporal, scored on a common scale [@zharnikov-2026-spectral-brand-theory-computational-framework] — and measured under the formal metric of that space [@zharnikov-2026-brand-space-geometry-formal-metric]. Second, a *monotone link*: the cohort's stated choice propensity for the brand within its category consideration set, $p(c,b) = g(m(c,b))$ with $g$ increasing — estimated isotonically, with no parametric form imposed. Third, an *induced-matrix bridge*: a propensity vector over a consideration set determines, under the zero-order Dirichlet parameterization, the entire closed-form purchase-level switching matrix, hence equilibrium shares and the double-jeopardy diagonal. If the link holds, the perceptual layer sets the matrix the stochastic tradition starts from.

Three objects fall out of the composition, and together they mark the paper's boundary — no adjacent account produces the three jointly, and the third is produced by none at all. (i) *Endogenous double jeopardy*: the double-jeopardy diagonal stops being a free empirical regularity and becomes an inherited signature — its shape is supplied by the Dirichlet parameterization the tradition itself uses, and the parameters behind it become measurable upstream. (ii) *A model-derived intermediate band*: the band of intermediate choice propensities — the practitioner's "movable middle," defined internally here and cited to nothing — is recovered as the image of intermediate spectral match under the monotone link, its population mass a function of cohort-profile dispersion rather than an imported probability cut. (iii) *Metameric equality*: brand pairs whose eight-dimensional profiles a cohort cannot distinguish [@zharnikov-2026-spectral-metamerism-brand-perception-projection] must receive statistically indistinguishable propensity vectors from that cohort, whatever their signal-level differences — a prediction unique to a measurement theory that knows when two brands are perceptually identical; random-utility estimation, congruity regressions, and equity hierarchies make none of the three. The payload is correspondingly concrete: if the link holds, a perceptual audit forecasts equilibrium share, the double-jeopardy diagonal, and the mass of the intermediate band before any purchase is observed.

The empirical claim is scoped precisely. All inference is at the cohort $\times$ brand cell — no individual-consumer inference is made or implied. The outcome is stated choice propensity in the stated-probability elicitation tradition [@juster-1966-consumer-buying-intentions-purchase], not behavior; the intentions-to-behavior calibration gap is a stated limitation [@morwitz-2007-when-purchase-intentions-predict]. Consideration sets are within-category by construction — spectral match does not rank cross-category alternatives. And the respondents are LLM cohort instruments, justified by the LLM-as-instrument convergence literature [@gilardi-2023-chatgpt-outperforms-crowd; @ziems-2024-can-large-language-models] — including convergence evidence specific to this paper's outcome, LLM synthetic respondents reproducing human purchase-intent distributions at 90% of human test-retest reliability [@maier-2025-llms-reproduce-purchase-intent]; whether the link transfers to human perceptual-choice processes is an explicitly open boundary question, not a claim of this paper. Within that scope the design is built to kill its own thesis: perception readings and propensity elicitations come from disjoint operator instances so a single generation cannot manufacture the correlation, a same-call contrast arm measures the common-method inflation directly, and four pre-registered kill conditions were stated before any call fired. None was triggered, and the measured inflation was effectively zero.

## Prior Art and Positioning

***The stochastic tradition and its exogenous inputs***

The tradition this paper engages is complementary, not rival. From the Markov appraisal [@ehrenberg-1965-appraisal-markov-brand-switching] through the Dirichlet synthesis [@goodhardt-1984-dirichlet-comprehensive-model] to the contemporary benchmarks [@ehrenberg-2004-understanding-brand-performance; @sharp-2010-how-brands-grow], its descriptive success is evidence that the equilibrium layer works. The open question is where its inputs come from. The Dirichlet models propensity heterogeneity across buyers without explaining it, in the same way aggregate tracking masks the cohort structure underneath it [@zharnikov-2026-non-ergodic-brand-perception-why]. The corpus position this paper answers constructively is that the switching table itself cannot be the explanation: the zero-order Dirichlet switching matrix is fast-mixing, with no slow modes in which perceptual structure could hide, so the propensities are set upstream [@zharnikov-2026-restoring-perceptual-separability-after-coherence]. Upstream is where this paper goes.

***Self-congruity and what it does not predict***

The closest prior art is the self-congruity tradition: congruence between a buyer's self-image and the brand's user imagery predicts preference and purchase intent, established across four decades of studies [@sirgy-1982-selfconcept-consumer-behavior] and validated predictively across methods [@sirgy-1997-predictive-validity-self-image-congruence]. The linking model is that insight upgraded to a measurement layer where it acquires stochastic-tradition consequences. The upgrade is threefold. The object of congruence changes: not a self-image scale but the full eight-dimensional spectral profile, measured under an explicit metric on the perception space. The predicted quantity changes: not mean preference but the entire within-set propensity vector — which is what the Dirichlet needs as input. And the consequences change: a propensity vector induces the switching matrix, so the congruity insight, restated spectrally, reaches equilibrium structure the congruity literature never touches — double jeopardy, share, the intermediate band, and the metameric-equality prediction of the next section. The same contrast covers the equity hierarchies: customer-based brand equity and its descendants run from associations through evaluative scales to intention [@aaker-1991-managing-brand-equity; @keller-1993-conceptualizing-measuring-managing], and geometric treatments of brand perception have historically stopped at representation rather than choice [@zharnikov-2026-geometric-approaches-brand-perception-critical]. None of these predicts the propensity *vector*, and none induces the matrix. Likewise, the random-utility and discrete-choice traditions solve the inverse problem — estimating preference parameters from observed choices; this paper runs the forward direction, predicting the choice-propensity inputs from an independent perceptual measurement, so the two are complements rather than competitors.

***The mechanism: processing fluency***

A monotone link invites the question of what carries it. The nominated mechanism is processing fluency: stimuli congruent with an activated schema are processed more easily, and the ease is experienced as positive evaluation of the stimulus — with the conceptual-fluency variant, congruence with expectation in context, the operative form for brand stimuli [@lee-labroo-2004-conceptual-perceptual-fluency-brand]. Read through the linking model, a brand whose profile matches the cohort's own profile is exactly a brand congruent with the cohort's activated schema, so fluency predicts the monotone shape of $g$ directly. It also yields an auxiliary prediction that generic congruence-liking does not: if fluency carries the link, congruence on the dimensions *salient* to the cohort should carry more of it than congruence on non-salient dimensions, because salient dimensions are where schema-congruence resolves the most processing. The pre-registered auxiliary test compares the link's strength under salience-weighted and unweighted match, $\Delta\tau_w = \tau_b(\text{weighted}) - \tau_b(\text{unweighted})$, with direction $\Delta\tau_w \geq 0$; a systematically negative $\Delta\tau_w$ among floor-passing operators would leave the link intact but the fluency account unsupported.

## The Linking Model

***The match statistic***

A cohort $c$ is a perceptual unit, not a demographic one: a set of observers characterized by a common observer spectral profile $\theta_c \in [1,10]^8$ on the eight dimensions of the perception space — Semiotic, Narrative, Ideological, Experiential, Social, Economic, Cultural, Temporal [@zharnikov-2026-spectral-brand-theory-computational-framework] — with cohort boundaries defined in the high-dimensional space itself [@zharnikov-2026-cohort-boundaries-high-dimensional-perception]. A brand $b$ occupies the same space through its measured profile $\beta_b$. Spectral match is the normalized similarity

$$m(c,b) = 1 - \frac{d(\theta_c, \beta_b)}{d_{\max}},$$

with $d$ the metric of the perception space [@zharnikov-2026-brand-space-geometry-formal-metric] (Euclidean primary; cosine and per-dimension salience-weighted forms as frozen variants) and $d_{\max}$ the metric's diameter on the profile domain. The statistic inherits its meaning from the metric: $m$ near 1 is a brand read as perceptually *own* by the cohort, $m$ near 0 a brand read as perceptually alien.

***The monotone link***

The first proposition is the link itself: at cohort level, stated choice propensity within a category consideration set is monotone increasing in spectral match,

$$p(c,b) = g(m(c,b)), \qquad g \ \text{increasing},$$

with $g$ estimated isotonically — no functional form is imposed at the primary test, because the claim is ordinal: better-matched brands receive higher stated propensity. The claim is falsifiable and its falsifier is pre-registered: the link is dead if the monotone association is absent (pooled $\tau_b < .20$, or a permutation $p$ at or above the pre-registered $\alpha = .05$) in both study categories among floor-passing operators, or if its sign flips between floor-passing operator families — a link that depends on which model family one asks is not a finding about perception.

***From propensities to the matrix***

The second component is analytic. Under the zero-order Dirichlet parameterization with concentration $S$ and share vector $s$, the purchase-level switching matrix has the closed form

$$P = \frac{I + S\,\mathbf{1}s^{\top}}{S+1},$$

with stationary distribution $s$, diagonal affine in share — the double-jeopardy signature — and spectral gap $S/(S+1)$ [@zharnikov-2026-restoring-perceptual-separability-after-coherence]. A cohort-weighted propensity vector over the consideration set therefore determines the switching structure outright (cohorts weighted equally: the designed cohorts carry no population masses and none are claimed). Map the measured propensities to $(S, s)$ — $s$ from the mean propensity shares, $S$ by moment-matching the across-cohort propensity dispersion to the Dirichlet variance relation — and the matrix, its equilibrium shares, and its double-jeopardy diagonal follow with no further empirical freedom. Composed with the monotone link, this is the paper's title claim: perception sets the matrix. The bridge carries its own internal falsifier — if induced matrices from measured propensity vectors systematically fail the double-jeopardy diagonal ordering that direct switching probes exhibit in the same data, the bridge fails as a description of those data.

***The intermediate band***

The third object is derived, not assumed. Because $g$ is monotone, the image of an intermediate interval of match values is an intermediate band of propensities, and the band's population mass equals the mass of cohorts whose match with the brand falls in that interval — a quantity governed by cohort-profile dispersion in the category. The intermediate-match band is thus a model-derived object: which cohorts sit at intermediate propensity, and how many, becomes a prediction of the perceptual layer rather than a descriptive cut at fixed probabilities. The pre-registered secondary analysis tests the dispersion claim directly: intermediate-band mass should track cohort-profile dispersion across the study categories.

***A sharp test: metameric equality***

The fourth proposition is the model's sharpest exposure. If propensity depends on the brand only through the match statistic, then two brands whose profiles a cohort cannot distinguish — a metameric pair, in the projection sense established for brand perception [@zharnikov-2026-spectral-metamerism-brand-perception-projection] — must receive statistically indistinguishable propensity vectors from that cohort, whatever their signal-level differences. No utility-based or congruity-based account makes this prediction, because none carries a criterion for when two brands are perceptually identical. The corollary bounds the thesis from above: metameric pairs (profile distance below the operator floor) showing propensity differences beyond the elicitation floor would demonstrate that the perceptual layer is not sufficient for the propensity input, whatever the monotone link's strength.

## Method: A Pre-Registered Two-Study Design

The design is pre-registered in full before any API call fires; the frozen protocol (instruments, floors, analysis rules, kill conditions) follows the pre-registered-instrument discipline of the corpus's measurement family [@zharnikov-2026az-prism-m-metamerism; @zharnikov-2026as-prism-structured-measurement]. Design constants: $K = 10$ cohorts per category; $B = 6$ synthetic brands (Study 1) or $5$ real brands per category (Study 2); three replicate calls per cell per operator.

Table 1: Pre-Registered Two-Study Design.

| | Study 1 — causal core | Study 2 — replication and boundary check |
|---|---|---|
| Brands | 6 synthetic within-category stimuli, profiles manipulated by design | 2 real categories $\times$ 5 real brands |
| Match $m(c,b)$ | Set experimentally (profile grid) | Measured in-study (reading operators) |
| Inference | Experimental monotone-link test | Observational replication |
| Role | Identification | External validity |

*Notes*: Operator floors are computed from the campaign's own reading arm; the known-profile recovery floor is grounded in the authored Study-1 target profiles, the only profile ground truth that exists by construction.

***Study 1: the causal core***

Study 1 removes the reverse path by construction. Six synthetic brands in one fictional category (specialty coffee roasters) are authored as stimulus packs — a short positioning page plus three artifact snippets — each written to a target eight-dimensional profile on a grid spanning match distances to the cohort set. Because the brands do not exist, no pre-existing familiarity or preference can drive both readings and propensities; spectral match is *set*, not observed. Stimulus authoring is itself gated: reading operators must recover the target profiles within tolerance before the propensity arm runs, and packs failing recovery are re-authored under log. The ten cohort persona specifications are observer spectral profiles chosen by maximin Latin hypercube over the profile domain — so match values span their range rather than clustering — each carrying a normalized per-dimension salience weight vector rendered into the persona text, used by the salience-weighted match variant and the fluency auxiliary. The space-filling design doubles as the variance-collapse guard: LLM respondents risk homogeneity [@jiang-2025-artificial-hivemind], and profile dispersion is imposed by design rather than hoped for.

***Study 2: replication and boundary check***

Study 2 asks whether the experimental link survives contact with real brands. Two real product categories of five brands each (fixed at design freeze; selected for genuine consideration-set membership, public-artifact richness, and profile dispersion in pilot readings) are read in-study: brand profiles $\beta_b$ from reading operators over public-artifact bundles, propensities from disjoint eliciting operators. The arm is observational — the reverse path returns as a live rival and is addressed by the frozen confound battery (brand fixed effects; familiarity and salience controls), not by assumption. Study 2 is framed as replication and boundary check of the Study-1 link; its secondary analyses (induced-matrix consistency, band mass) are reported in full in the web appendix.

***Elicitation instruments***

Two instruments, strictly separated. The *reading* instrument is the standard eight-dimension perception reading: an 8-vector on $[1,10]$ in canonical dimension order, cohort-unconditioned for brand profiles (the brand's profile is an object-level measurement) and cohort-conditioned only to validate each cohort's own profile; the reading methodology descends from the corpus's perception-reading lineage [@zharnikov-2026ax-brand-spectrometer]. The *propensity* instrument is a study instrument built for this paper — it is not a capability claim of the perception-reading instrument, whose measurement claim remains perception and valence only. Eliciting operator instances receive the cohort persona specification and the category consideration set and produce: a constant-sum allocation ("of the next 10 category purchases this cohort makes, how many go to each brand"; sums to 10, sum-check enforced) as the primary propensity measure; a per-brand eleven-point purchase-probability scale in the stated-probability tradition [@juster-1966-consumer-buying-intentions-purchase] as a calibration cross-check; and a descriptive switching probe (current brand given, next-purchase probabilities over the set) feeding the induced-matrix illustration only. The constant-sum form presumes near-exhaustive consideration sets at the category grain studied; outside-set leakage is checked in pilots and carried as a limitation.

***Instrument separation and the same-call contrast***

The central validity threat is that an LLM generation is coherent: a single call asked for both a perception reading and a propensity could manufacture the correlation internally, and the design treats this as the thesis's leading rival rather than a nuisance. Under the separation protocol, reading calls and eliciting calls run in separate sessions on disjoint operator instances sharing no conversational context — the only shared inputs are the persona specification and the stimulus materials — following the corpus's cross-operator extraction-separation discipline. The match statistic entering the primary test is computed exclusively from reading-arm output, the propensity exclusively from eliciting-arm output, so no single generation produces both sides of the correlation. A deliberate *same-call contrast arm* then measures what separation removed: one call produces both reading and propensity, and the common-method inflation is reported as $\Delta\tau = \tau_{\text{same-call}} - \tau_{\text{separated}}$. The corresponding kill condition is absolute: if the separated-arm link is null while the same-call link is positive, the "link" is intra-generation coherence and the thesis is dead.

***Operators, floors, and sample size***

The operator pool spans three model families with two models each (Table 2), model identifiers pinned and decoding parameters frozen at design freeze; two reserve operators from a fourth family are pre-registered with a frozen replacement rule (hard API unavailability only — floor failure is demotion, never substitution); every call is JSONL-logged (prompt, parameters, raw response, parse, timestamps, model identifier) per the corpus's call-logging discipline.

Four operator floors are frozen per operator, computed from the campaign's own arms: F1, reading test–retest ICC(2,1) $\geq .60$ on the operator's repeated brand readings; F2, propensity coherence — constant-sum checks pass in at least 95% of calls and Juster-scale versus constant-sum rank agreement $\tau \geq .5$ within operator; F3, known-profile recovery — the operator's mean Study-1 pack readings recover the authored target profiles within mean absolute deviation $\leq 1.5$ scale points per dimension (the authored targets are the only profile ground truth that exists by construction; no externally asserted profile is used as a reference); F4, refusal or malformed-output rate $\leq 5\%$. Stated-propensity stability under the frozen instrument is thus a checked assumption, not a presumed one.

Sample size follows a fixed-seed simulation (companion script, seed 20260712; 1,000 simulations $\times$ 1,000 permutations): at 60 cells (Study 1) with the within-cohort permutation null, a true monotone association of $\tau = .30$ is detected at $\alpha = .05$ with power .952 per operator (.910 at Study 2's 50 cells); at the pre-registered floor $\tau = .20$, per-operator power is .702, rising to .980 pooled across four floor-passing operators (.952 pooled at 50 cells). With the frozen arm sizes and six operators, the campaign is roughly 400 primary calls per category across both arms, plus per-operator calibration floors.

***Frozen analysis and kill conditions***

The primary statistic is Kendall $\tau_b$ between $m(c,b)$ and $p(c,b)$ over cohort $\times$ brand cells, per category $\times$ operator, cell values taken as medians over replicates; the null is a within-cohort brand-label permutation (10,000 draws, fixed seed); pooling is the median $\tau_b$ across floor-passing operators; and the category-level conclusion requires a pooled permutation $p$ below $\alpha = .05$ *and* pooled $\tau_b \geq .20$, with exact $p$ reported to three digits and $\tau_b$ with per-category isotonic $R^2$ as effect sizes. Direction is pre-registered positive; a significant negative pooled link is a falsification, not a finding to reinterpret. Secondary analyses, all labeled secondary: the common-method inflation $\Delta\tau$; the fluency auxiliary $\Delta\tau_w$; the induced-matrix layer (map Study-2 pooled propensities to $(S, s)$, form the closed-form matrix, and check its double-jeopardy diagonal ordering against the descriptive switching probes); and intermediate-band mass as a function of cohort-profile dispersion. The frozen robustness list admits no post-hoc additions: cosine in place of Euclidean match; per-dimension salience weights; Spearman $\rho$ in place of $\tau_b$; leave-one-brand-out; leave-one-operator-out; brand fixed effects and familiarity/salience controls in Study 2; and a random-profile falsification — the pipeline's own placebo test — in which measured profiles are replaced by random 8-vectors; an association surviving that replacement would indicate an artifact in the analysis pipeline, not a finding. All cells run to completion; missing cells after retries are reported and excluded listwise.

Four kill conditions are pre-registered. K1, no link: the monotone association absent in both categories among floor-passing operators. K2, operator-unstable link: sign flips between floor-passing operator families. K3, common-method artifact: the separated-arm link null while the same-call link is positive. K4, instrument failure: floors fail for the majority of the pool. Any of the four ends the thesis as posed.

## Results

The pre-registered campaign executed on 2026-07-12 under the frozen protocol: 1,292 logged model API calls, all 1,224 planned measurement cells completed, with two harness token-cap amendments and one supplementary script (the pre-registered metameric check) recorded in the frozen protocol's append-only amendment log; none altered the instruments, prompts, design, or analysis rules. All values below are the frozen estimator's output (seed 20260712; 10,000-draw permutation nulls).

***The monotone link***

Five of six operators passed all four floors; claude-sonnet-5 was demoted on F2 (sum-check rate .90 against the .95 floor) and F4 (failure rate .077 against the .05 floor) — the failures trace to a thinking-token cap interaction on the longest Study-1 prompts, recorded as an amendment and reported here rather than repaired retroactively; the demotion rule is frozen and was applied as written. Kill condition K4 was not triggered.

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

| Quick-service coffee | 2 | .214 | < .001 | .118 | −.006 | −.071 | yes |

*Notes*: Pooled $\tau_b$ = median across the five floor-passing operators; permutation $p$ one-sided (pre-registered positive direction), shared within-cohort brand-label permutations. $\Delta\tau$ = same-call minus separated-arm association (common-method inflation). $\Delta\tau_w$ = salience-weighted minus unweighted match association (fluency auxiliary, pre-registered direction $\geq 0$).

The experimental category delivers the link as pre-registered: pooled $\tau_b = .323$ (per-operator range .072 to .355), permutation $p < .001$, both category-level criteria met. Quick-service coffee replicates it observationally ($\tau_b = .214$, $p < .001$), sitting just above the pre-registered $\tau_b \geq .20$ floor. Athletic footwear does not: $\tau_b = .018$, $p = .534$, with every robustness variant near zero — a boundary result taken up below and in the Discussion. Because the link is present in the experimental category and one real category, kill condition K1 is not triggered; all family-pooled associations carry the same sign (K2 clear).

The same-call contrast arm delivers the design's sharpest methodological number: common-method inflation is measured at $\Delta\tau = -.002$ (Study 1), $-.006$ (quick-service coffee), and $+.035$ (footwear). The separated-arm link equals the same-call link where the link exists — the association is not manufactured inside single generations, and kill condition K3 is not triggered.

The fluency auxiliary fails. Salience-weighted match predicts propensity *worse* than unweighted match exactly where the link is strongest: $\Delta\tau_w = -.196$ in Study 1, significant against the pre-registered direction (one-sided permutation $p = .002$); $-.071$ ($p = .179$) in quick-service coffee; $+.020$ ($p = .302$) in footwear. The salience-congruence form of the fluency mechanism is unsupported; the monotone link itself is unaffected, and the mechanism question is reopened rather than papered over.

***The induced matrix***

Mapping the measured propensity vectors through the moment-matching rule gives concentration parameters $S$ of 11.8, 21.2, and 29.6 for the three categories (share vectors in the web appendix), and the closed-form matrix's double-jeopardy diagonal follows. Checked against the descriptive switching probes, the induced diagonal ordering matches the observed repeat-propensity ordering strongly in the experimental category ($\tau = .867$), weakly in footwear ($\tau = .4$), and not at all in quick-service coffee ($\tau = .0$), where two smaller brands show repeat propensities above their share rank — the classic niche-loyalty deviation from the double-jeopardy pattern. The bridge holds where match is experimentally set and is mixed observationally; its internal falsifier (systematic failure of the ordering) is not met.

***The metameric bound***

The metameric-equality check was exercisable and is the campaign's sharpest bound. Six operator-level metameric pairs emerged (median-profile distance below the operator's own reading floor), and in all six the median cohort propensity difference *exceeds* the elicitation floor. The cleanest case is Nike–Adidas: profile distance 1.41–2.69 against reading floors of 2.18–2.92 for two operators, yet a median propensity difference of .10 against elicitation floors of .03–.07. Where real brand profiles converge to perceptual indistinguishability, propensity differences survive — the perceptual layer is not *sufficient* for the propensity input there, exactly as the corollary's falsifier anticipated, and exactly in the category where the monotone link failed.

***Robustness***

The frozen list behaves consistently with the primary picture. In the two link-bearing categories: cosine match, $\tau = .381$ and $.207$; Spearman $\rho = .413$ and $.297$; leave-one-brand-out pooled $\tau_b$ spans .222–.352 (Study 1) and .178–.318 (quick-service coffee); leave-one-operator-out spans .279–.326 and .177–.256; brand fixed effects — which absorb brand-level familiarity and salience — leave within-brand associations of .196 and .255. In footwear every variant stays near zero (cosine .085; Spearman .052; fixed effects .125). The random-profile falsification destroys the link everywhere (pooled $\tau$ of .034, .084, .038): the pipeline does not manufacture associations. Intermediate-band masses are .477, .408, and .040; because the space-filling design fixed cohort-profile dispersion to a nearly constant 10.65–10.81 across categories, the pre-registered dispersion-tracking secondary had no leverage this campaign — band mass instead tracked link strength, and the dispersion prediction awaits a design that varies dispersion deliberately.

## Alternative Explanations

Three rivals were treated as live and adjudicated by design rather than argument. The *common-method rival* — the association is manufactured within single LLM generations — is now rejected on measurement: under instrument separation the two sides of the correlation come from disjoint operator instances, and the same-call contrast arm measured the inflation at effectively zero in all three categories (Table 3). A manufactured link would appear in the same-call arm and vanish under separation; the observed pattern is the opposite of the artifact signature. The *silicon-artifact rival* — the link is a regularity of LLM representations that does not transfer to human perceptual-choice processes — is scoped, not resolved: every claim is bounded to LLM-cohort regularities, the LLM-as-instrument literature motivates but does not guarantee transfer [@gilardi-2023-chatgpt-outperforms-crowd; @ziems-2024-can-large-language-models; @maier-2025-llms-reproduce-purchase-intent], homogeneity risk is separately mitigated by the space-filling cohort design and operator diversity [@jiang-2025-artificial-hivemind], and human replication is recorded as an open boundary question. The *reverse-path rival* — familiarity or pre-existing preference drives both readings and propensities — is ruled out in Study 1 by construction (synthetic brands carry no priors, and the experimental link is the campaign's strongest) and is bounded in Study 2's observational arm by the frozen controls: brand fixed effects, which absorb brand-level familiarity and salience, leave the within-brand associations essentially intact (Robustness). The fourth rival, that the congruity tradition already covers the finding, is answered in the positioning section: that tradition predicts mean preference from congruence scales; it does not predict the propensity vector, induce the matrix, derive the band, or make the metameric-equality prediction.

## Discussion

***Where the link holds and where it stops***

The campaign's most instructive pattern is the coherence between its positive and negative results. The link is strongest where spectral match is set experimentally on dispersed profiles (Study 1), survives observationally where real profiles still disperse (quick-service coffee, from third-wave premium to drive-through value), and vanishes in athletic footwear — the category whose leading brands turned out to be perceptually metameric to the reading operators. The metameric check makes the connection explicit: where Nike and Adidas are indistinguishable on the eight-dimensional reading, their propensity difference survives anyway, so whatever separates them for a cohort is not in the measured perceptual state. Perception sets the matrix where perception still separates the brands; where profiles converge, the propensity inputs are set by something the perceptual layer does not carry — habit, availability, distribution, or dimensions the instrument does not measure. The boundary is itself measurable: profile dispersion among a category's brands is an observable, so the linking model comes with a pre-condition that can be checked before it is applied. The failed fluency auxiliary sharpens the same lesson from the mechanism side — salience-weighted congruence predicts *worse* than whole-profile congruence, so the link, where it holds, runs through the profile as a whole rather than through the dimensions a cohort says it cares about.

***The intermediate band as a prediction target***

The practitioner conversation around intermediate-propensity buyers has run ahead of measurement: the band is real in purchase data, but existing treatments define it by fiat — a probability interval imposed on the choice distribution. Under the linking model the band is *derived*: it is the image of intermediate spectral match, its membership is predictable from cohort profiles, and its mass moves with cohort-profile dispersion in the category. That converts a descriptive cut into two measurable questions — which cohorts sit at intermediate match with the brand, and how dispersed the category's cohort profiles are — both answerable from the perceptual layer before any purchase is observed. This campaign measured the band masses but could not test the dispersion prediction (Robustness): the space-filling design fixed cohort dispersion nearly constant across categories, and the claim awaits a design that varies dispersion deliberately. The register is deliberately metrological: the claim is that the band can be measured and predicted, not a prescription for what to do with it.

***What the stochastic tradition gains***

Nothing in this paper competes with the Dirichlet; the point is what the composition adds to it. If the monotone link holds, the tradition's exogenous inputs become measurable outputs of an upstream perceptual layer: category equilibria — shares, double jeopardy, the switching table — become downstream consequences of profile geometry, and interventions on perception acquire a modeled path to equilibrium consequences. The metameric-equality corollary sharpens the measurement stakes: if two brands' profiles are perceptually indistinguishable to a cohort, no propensity daylight between them is available to that cohort, so signal-level differentiation that fails to move the profile is — for that cohort — propensity-inert. And the static composition is deliberately the first step: propensity drift as perception moves is the corpus's dynamics layer and the natural follow-on, out of scope here.

## Limitations

Four limitations bound the claims. First, *LLM-cohort respondents only* — the most consequential boundary: the link is established as an LLM-cohort regularity; its transfer to human perceptual-choice processes is an open boundary question, and no human claim is made. The claims are further bounded by the campaign's stimulus and elicitation language, fixed at design freeze, with any non-English market requiring fully translated instruments before the boundary extends to it. Second, *stated propensity only*: the outcome is stated choice propensity in the Juster tradition, and the intentions-to-behavior calibration gap is inherited in full [@morwitz-2007-when-purchase-intentions-predict]; behavioral validation against panel switching data is future work, not a claim. Third, *a static link*: the design measures the perception-propensity link at a point in time and defers its dynamics. Fourth, *consideration-set exhaustiveness*: the constant-sum instrument approximates propensities only where the elicited set covers the category grain studied; leakage outside the set is checked in pilots and reported.
