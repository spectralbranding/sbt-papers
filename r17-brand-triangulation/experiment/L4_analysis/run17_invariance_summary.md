# Run 17: Cross-Cohort Measurement Invariance — Summary

**Date**: 2026-04-16
**Data source**: Experiment 1 (741 baseline records)
**Additional API calls**: 0

## Hypothesis Results

### H1: Configural Invariance — CONFIRMED

No dimensions systematically zeroed by any cohort. All 10 cohorts produce non-zero weights on all 8 dimensions. PRISM-B's 8-factor structure holds across behaviorally distinct observer types.

### H2: Metric Invariance — FAILS (as predicted by SBT)

Overall median within-brand Spearman rho = .548, below the .70 threshold.

| Brand | Median rho | Mean rho | Min rho | Pairs |
|-------|-----------|----------|---------|-------|
| Hermes | .452 | .439 | -.167 | 45 |
| IKEA | .452 | .493 | -.048 | 45 |
| Patagonia | .635 | .645 | .238 | 45 |
| Erewhon | .524 | .437 | -.524 | 45 |
| Tesla | .548 | .523 | -.143 | 45 |

Patagonia shows the strongest cross-cohort agreement (median rho = .635) — its environmental mission creates convergent perception. Hermes and IKEA show the weakest agreement (median rho = .452) — these brands are perceived through fundamentally different dimensional lenses depending on observer type.

### H3: Scalar Invariance — CONFIRMED (fails, as SBT predicts)

All 8 dimensions show significant Kruskal-Wallis tests (p < .001). Cohorts assign systematically different absolute weights — this is the observer heterogeneity that SBT's mathematical framework is designed to model.

## Cross-Cohort Variance Ranking

Most observer-dependent dimensions (highest cross-cohort variance):
1. **Economic** (variance = .00395) — observer's relationship with value is most variable
2. **Ideological** (variance = .00119) — values/ethics perception is observer-dependent
3. **Social** (variance = .00090) — social signaling interpretation varies by cohort

Least observer-dependent:
- **Cultural** (variance = .00014) — cultural relevance perception is most stable

## Key Findings

1. **Configural invariance holds**: PRISM-B's structure is robust — all cohorts engage with all 8 dimensions.
2. **Metric invariance fails**: dimension rank-ordering differs across cohorts, confirming that observer perspective shapes dimensional weighting. This is the core SBT prediction.
3. **Scalar invariance fails**: absolute weights differ significantly, providing the strongest evidence for observer-dependent perception.
4. **Economic is the "most subjective" dimension**: highest cross-cohort variance and largest H statistic. How observers relate to value/price is the most observer-dependent aspect of brand perception.
5. **Cultural is the "most objective" dimension**: lowest cross-cohort variance. Cultural meaning may be more inherent to the brand than projected by the observer.
