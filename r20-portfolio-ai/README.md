# R20: Spectral Immunity: Portfolio Framing Does Not Alter AI Brand Perception

**Citation key**: 2026ab | **DOI**: [10.5281/zenodo.19555282](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19555282) | **Status**: Working paper v1.0

## Paper

Zharnikov, D. (2026ab). Spectral Immunity: Portfolio Framing Does Not Alter AI Brand Perception. Working Paper. https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19555282

## Abstract

This paper tests whether corporate portfolio framing alters AI brand perception. Nine brands across three portfolios (LVMH, Unilever, Procter & Gamble) are rated under solo and portfolio conditions by 7 large language models (378 total observations, 355 successfully parsed). Results show near-zero effect of portfolio framing on dimensional concentration. The awareness gate mechanism required for portfolio interference is permanently saturated in AI observers yet produces no measurable interference -- a finding termed "spectral immunity."
