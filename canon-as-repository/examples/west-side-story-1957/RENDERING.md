# Fork: West Side Story (1957)

## Fork Type
Full fork — this is not a rendering of Romeo and Juliet. It is a fork that transforms
the spec while preserving the structural skeleton. The character names, dialogue, setting,
and world surface are all replaced. What is preserved is the architecture.

This distinction matters. Luhrmann kept Shakespeare's words and replaced the world.
Bernstein, Sondheim, and Robbins kept Shakespeare's structure and replaced everything else.

## Key Divergences from Spec

**Maria survives.** This is the most significant spec divergence. Juliet's self-authored death
is a spec constraint ("death must be by her own hand — a deliberate choice, not an accident").
West Side Story overrides this: Maria lives. The structural consequence is that her survival
becomes the indictment — she must live with the loss, which is arguably a different form
of the same punishment.

**No institutional reconciliation.** Romeo and Juliet ends with the Prince presiding over
the families' acknowledgment of their failure. West Side Story ends with Maria alone,
with the Jets and Sharks carrying Tony's body together — a visual gesture toward understanding,
but not a formal reconciliation. The spec's feud-resolution constraint is modified.

**The feud is made politically legible.** Shakespeare's feud is ancient and origin-less.
The Jets/Sharks conflict is rooted in racism, immigration anxiety, and economic competition.
This is a spec enrichment: the structural function is preserved (feud is structural,
cannot be resolved by individual action) but the cause is now specified and political.

## Why This Example Matters

West Side Story demonstrates that a fork can modify the specification itself while preserving
enough of the structural skeleton to be recognizable as the same work.

It is not a rendering of Romeo and Juliet in any standard sense. You cannot validate it
against the Romeo and Juliet spec and expect compliance. But you can compare the two specs
and identify exactly what was preserved, what was modified, and what was replaced.

This is what version control makes visible. Without explicit specs, we say "West Side Story
is based on Romeo and Juliet" — a vague claim. With specs, we can say: the structural skeleton
is preserved at the level of plot function; the character spec is replaced; the world spec is
replaced; two of the four spec constraints at the ending are overridden; one spec constraint
(feud cannot be resolved by individual action) is preserved in modified form.

That is a more useful description.
